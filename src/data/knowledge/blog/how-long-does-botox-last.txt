How Long Does Botox Last?

Lumina Aesthetics — Raleigh, North Carolina

Introduction

“How long does Botox last?” is one of the most common questions we get. The short answer: it varies, but many people see results for three to four months before they choose to return. Here’s what to expect and what affects longevity.

Typical Timeline

Most clients notice softening within a few days, with full effect around 7–14 days. The effect is temporary; over time, muscle activity gradually returns and lines may reappear. For many people, that means returning every 3–4 months for neurotoxin. Some may stretch to 4–5 months depending on area, dose, and metabolism.

What Affects How Long It Lasts

Metabolism, muscle strength, area treated, and dose all play a role. Areas that move more may “wear off” a bit sooner. Consistency of treatment can also affect how things look over time—some clients find that with regular treatment, they need a bit less or can space visits slightly longer. We do not promise a specific duration; we give you a realistic range.

When to Plan Your Next Visit

We will suggest a follow-up window based on your response. Many clients book their next appointment before they leave so they stay on schedule. You can always adjust the date if needed.

Summary

Neurotoxin results typically last a few months. Plan on a consultation to discuss your goals and a realistic timeline for maintenance at Lumina.
