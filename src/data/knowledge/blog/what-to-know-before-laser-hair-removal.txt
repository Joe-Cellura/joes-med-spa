What to Know Before Laser Hair Removal

Lumina Aesthetics — Raleigh, North Carolina

Introduction

Laser hair removal can significantly reduce unwanted hair over time, but it requires a commitment to a series of treatments and proper preparation. Here’s what to know before you start at Lumina.

It’s a Series, Not One Visit

Hair grows in cycles. One session will not remove all hair permanently. We typically recommend a series of sessions—often 6–8 or more—spaced 4–8 weeks apart. Results vary; many clients see long-lasting reduction and may need occasional maintenance sessions.

Skin and Hair Type Matter

Laser works by targeting pigment in the hair. It is most effective when there is contrast between hair and skin. We assess your skin type and hair color at your consultation to determine candidacy and set appropriate settings. Newer technology allows us to treat a broader range of skin types safely—we will tell you what to expect.

Shave Before Treatment

Shaving before your appointment is standard. Do not wax or pluck; that removes the follicle and reduces effectiveness. We will give you clear instructions before your first session.

Avoid Sun

Tanned skin can increase the risk of side effects. We will ask you to avoid sun exposure and use sunscreen as we advise before and between sessions. If you have a tan, we may need to reschedule.

What to Expect During and After

Treatment can feel like warmth or a snapping sensation. We use cooling to keep you comfortable. Afterward, skin may be slightly red or sensitive for a few hours. We will provide aftercare and schedule your next session.

Summary

Laser hair removal is a commitment. Come for a consultation so we can assess your skin and hair, explain the series, and prepare you for your first visit.
