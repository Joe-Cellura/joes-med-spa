Lumina Aesthetics — Acne and Acne-Related Treatments

Overview

Lumina offers treatments that can support clearer, healthier-looking skin. We do not diagnose acne as a medical condition; that is the role of a physician or dermatologist. We provide facials, chemical peels, microneedling, and other professional treatments that may help with congestion, texture, and the appearance of acne and post-acne marks. All recommendations are personalized after we assess your skin and goals.

Who It's For

Adults who want professional support for oily or congested skin, occasional breakouts, or the appearance of acne scars or redness. Good candidates are generally in good health and can follow a consistent skincare and treatment plan. We may recommend you see a dermatologist for diagnosis or prescription options; we work within our scope.

Common Goals

Reduce congestion and breakouts, improve texture and the look of acne scars, calm redness, support a clearer complexion alongside home care.

What Treatment May Involve

Depending on your assessment, we may recommend facials (including deep cleansing or Hydrafacial), chemical peels, or microneedling. Each has different sensation, downtime, and frequency. We will explain what we recommend and why.

Typical Appointment Length

Varies by treatment—from 30 minutes for some facials to 60 minutes for microneedling. Consultation time is included for first-time or changing plans.

Downtime

Depends on the treatment. Facials may have minimal downtime; peels and microneedling can involve redness or peeling for a few days. We will outline what to expect.

Results Timeline

Results are often gradual. Consistency with both in-office treatments and home care matters. We may recommend a series of treatments and a simple home routine. We do not guarantee clear skin; we aim to support improvement.

Pricing Range

Pricing varies by service. Facials may start in the range of approximately $150–250; peels and microneedling are priced as listed in their respective service descriptions. We will recommend a plan and quote at your consultation.

Consultation Guidance

We assess your skin type, concerns, and current routine. We may ask about your history with acne and any products or prescriptions you use. We recommend treatments that fit your skin and explain why a personalized plan matters—what works for one person may not for another.

Aftercare Notes

We provide product and routine guidance to support results and avoid irritation. This may include gentle cleansing, non-comedogenic moisturizer, sunscreen, and avoiding picking or over-treating at home.

When This May Be a Good Fit

When you want professional support for congestion or the appearance of acne and post-acne marks, and when you are willing to follow a plan. When you understand we do not replace a dermatologist for diagnosis or prescription care.

When a Consultation Matters Most

Always for acne-related concerns. Skin type, sensitivity, and goals vary; we need to see your skin and recommend the right combination of treatments and home care.
