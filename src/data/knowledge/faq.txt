Lumina Aesthetics — Frequently Asked Questions

Who performs treatments at Lumina?

All injectable and laser treatments are performed or directly supervised by licensed medical professionals. Facials and certain skin treatments are performed by licensed aestheticians under medical oversight. You will be told who is treating you before any service.

Is there a medical director?

Yes. A board-certified physician serves as Medical Director and oversees protocols, safety, and complex cases. She also performs or supervises injectable treatments.

Do you focus on natural-looking results?

Yes. We aim for results that look like you—refreshed or refined, not overdone. We use conservative dosing and technique where appropriate and will tell you if we think less is more for your goals.

Is there downtime?

It depends on the treatment. Neuromodulators often have minimal downtime. Fillers can involve a few days of swelling or bruising. Laser and some facials may cause redness for a day or two. We will explain what to expect for the specific treatment you choose.

How is pricing handled?

Pricing is discussed during your consultation. You will receive an estimate before any treatment. Injectable pricing may be per unit or per area; skin and body treatments may be per session or as a series. We are transparent; there are no hidden fees.

What happens at the first visit?

Your first visit is a consultation. We review your goals, medical history, and the areas you want to address. We recommend options, explain downtime and results, and provide pricing. You can book treatment for the same day in some cases, or schedule a separate visit—your choice.

How do you decide what treatment is right for me?

We consider your goals, anatomy, skin condition, timeline, and comfort level. We recommend what fits you and will tell you if we think something is not a good fit. We do not push services that do not make sense for you.

How long do results last?

It varies by treatment. Neuromodulators often last three to four months for many people. Fillers can last several months to a year or more depending on product and area. Skin treatments may require maintenance sessions. We will give you a realistic range during your consultation.

Can I be treated the same day as my consultation?

Sometimes. For neuromodulator, same-day treatment may be an option if you are a good candidate and have had time to consider the plan. For filler or more involved treatments, we often recommend a separate visit. The provider will tell you what is appropriate.

What should I expect after injectables?

You may have mild redness, swelling, or small bumps at the injection sites. These usually resolve within a few hours to a few days. Bruising is possible, especially with filler. We will give you aftercare instructions. Avoid rubbing the area and follow any activity restrictions we provide.

I have an event in a few weeks. When should I come in?

For injectables, we generally recommend at least two weeks before a major event so any bruising or swelling can resolve. Some clients prefer a month. For facials or Hydrafacial, a few days to a week before is often fine. We can help you choose a treatment and date.

Who is a good candidate?

Candidacy depends on the treatment and your health history. We review medical history, medications, and goals during the consultation. Some conditions or medications may limit options. We will tell you clearly if something is not recommended for you.

Do you treat everyone?

We treat within our scope and only when it is safe and appropriate. We do not treat if we believe it is not in your best interest. We will explain why if we decline or suggest an alternative.

What about aftercare?

We provide written and verbal aftercare instructions for each treatment. This may include avoiding sun, certain products, or strenuous activity for a period. Following aftercare helps results and reduces risk of complications.

Can I do multiple treatments in one visit?

Sometimes. It depends on the treatments and your tolerance. We may recommend spacing some services to manage downtime or to see how you respond. The provider will outline a plan during your consultation.

Do you offer financing?

We can share current payment and financing options at the time of your visit. Payment is due at the time of service unless other arrangements have been made.

What if I am not sure I want treatment?

That is fine. The consultation is a chance to learn and decide. You are never obligated to book treatment. You can go home, think about it, and follow up when you are ready.

How do I book?

You can book a consultation through our website or by calling the clinic. We will confirm your appointment and send any intake forms or instructions before your visit.

Where is Lumina located?

Lumina Aesthetics is located in Raleigh, North Carolina. Full address and directions are on our website and can be confirmed when you book.

What are your hours?

Our hours are listed on our website and can be confirmed when you book. We offer weekday and limited weekend availability.

Can I bring someone to my consultation?

Yes. You may bring a support person if you prefer. Please let us know in advance if you have any accessibility or accommodation needs.
