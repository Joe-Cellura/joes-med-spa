Lumina Aesthetics — Consultation Process

What Happens During a First Consultation

Your first visit is a consultation, not necessarily a treatment appointment. You will check in, complete any intake forms, and then meet with a team member—often the consultation coordinator first, then a clinical provider. The provider will discuss your goals, review your medical history and current skincare or medications, and examine the areas you are interested in treating. They will explain which options make sense for you, what to expect in terms of downtime and results, and how pricing works.

Treatment Planning

Treatment planning is collaborative. The provider will recommend options based on your goals, anatomy, skin condition, timeline, and comfort level. They may suggest starting with one treatment and adding others over time, or they may recommend a single service. The plan is tailored to you; we do not use a standard package for everyone.

How Pricing Is Discussed

Pricing is discussed during the consultation. You will receive an estimate for the services recommended. Injectable pricing often depends on units or areas treated; skin and body treatments may be quoted per session or as a series. We do not hide costs. If you want to stage treatment over several visits, we can outline what that would look like and what to budget.

Same-Day Treatment

Same-day treatment is sometimes possible—for example, if you are a good candidate for neuromodulator and you have had time to consider the plan. It is never required. Some clients prefer to go home, think about it, and book a follow-up. For fillers or more involved treatments, we often recommend a separate treatment visit so you are not rushed. The provider will tell you what is appropriate for your situation.

Goals, Anatomy, Timeline, and Downtime

The consultation covers your goals (what you want to improve or maintain), your anatomy (how your face or body responds to treatment), your timeline (upcoming events, schedule), and downtime (how much recovery you can accommodate). These factors shape the recommendation. We will not suggest a treatment that does not fit your timeline or comfort level.

Safety and Candidacy

We screen for safety. The provider will ask about medical history, allergies, medications, and past reactions. Some conditions or medications may mean we cannot offer certain treatments, or we may need to adjust the plan. We will tell you clearly if something is not a good fit. We do not treat when it is not safe or appropriate.

Expectation-Setting

We set realistic expectations. Results vary by person. We explain typical outcomes, how long results tend to last, and what maintenance might look like. We do not guarantee specific results. We also explain possible side effects, such as bruising or swelling, so you can plan accordingly.

Follow-Up

After the consultation, you can book a treatment visit, schedule a follow-up consultation, or take time to decide. We are happy to answer follow-up questions by phone or email. If you proceed with treatment, we will review aftercare and any next steps before you leave.
