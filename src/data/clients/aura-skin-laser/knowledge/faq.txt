Laser Hair Reduction

Q: Is laser hair reduction painful?
A: Aura uses the Motus AZ+ laser, which is designed to minimize discomfort. Most clients describe the sensation as mild warmth. The Motus AZ+ is significantly more comfortable than older laser systems.

Q: How many sessions will I need?
A: Most clients need 6 to 12 sessions for optimal results, depending on hair color, skin tone, hormone levels, and the area being treated. Many clients see smoother, slower-growing hair after just 1 to 3 sessions.

Q: How do I prepare for laser hair reduction?
A: Shave the area 24 hours before. Do not wax, tweeze, or thread for 4 weeks prior. Avoid sun exposure and tanning before your session.

Q: Do you treat all skin tones?
A: Yes. The Motus AZ+ is safe and effective for all skin tones, including darker complexions that were previously difficult or unsafe to treat with older laser technology.

Q: Can I get laser hair reduction if I have dark skin?
A: Yes. The Motus AZ+ was specifically designed to be effective and safe for darker skin tones. Dipna will assess your skin before treatment and customize the protocol for your specific complexion.

Skin Treatments

Q: What is Moveo GLO?
A: Moveo GLO combines three laser treatments in one session: pigmentation reduction (Moveo PL), skin revitalization (Moveo SR), and vascular treatment (Moveo VL). It is ideal for clients wanting comprehensive improvement with fewer visits.

Q: How many sessions are needed for skin revitalization or dark spot reduction?
A: Results vary, but most clients see meaningful improvement after 3 sessions. A package of 3 is the most common starting point. Some clients see changes after their first session, particularly with dark spot treatments.

Q: Is Skinwave right for sensitive skin?
A: Yes. The Skinwave Aqua Facial is gentle enough for sensitive skin and works well for oily, dry, acne-prone, and combination skin types.

Q: How often should I get a Skinwave Aqua Facial?
A: Once a month for maintenance. For event prep, book 3 to 5 days before to allow any mild redness to settle.
