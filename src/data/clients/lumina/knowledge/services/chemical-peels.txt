Lumina Aesthetics — Chemical Peels

Overview

Chemical peels use solutions applied to the skin to exfoliate and improve tone, texture, and the appearance of fine lines, acne, or pigment. Peels range from light (minimal downtime) to deeper (more downtime and more significant results). At Lumina we select peel type and depth based on your skin, goals, and tolerance.

Who It's For

Adults who want to improve skin tone, texture, acne, or pigment and who can follow pre- and post-care. Good candidates are in good skin health and not pregnant or nursing. We assess your skin and any sensitivities before recommending a peel.

Common Goals

Brighten skin, improve texture, reduce the look of fine lines, address acne or post-acne marks, even out tone, boost glow.

What Treatment Feels Like

A solution is applied to the skin. You may feel tingling or warmth. Depth of peel affects sensation and downtime. We use appropriate neutralization and aftercare. Deeper peels involve more visible peeling and recovery.

Typical Appointment Length

30–60 minutes including consultation, application, and aftercare instructions. Deeper peels may require a longer visit and follow-up check.

Downtime

Varies by peel depth. Light peels may have minimal downtime—slight flaking or redness for a few days. Medium or deeper peels can involve several days of peeling and sensitivity. We will explain what to expect and when you can resume normal activities and makeup.

Results Timeline

Skin may look refreshed soon after a light peel. Deeper peels reveal new skin over one to two weeks. Multiple light or medium peels can be done in series for cumulative benefit. We will recommend a schedule based on your goals.

Pricing Range

At Lumina, chemical peels are typically in the range of approximately $150–450+ per session depending on peel type and depth. We will quote at your consultation.

Consultation Guidance

We review your skin type, goals, and any history of sensitivity or scarring. We recommend the appropriate peel depth and pre-care (e.g., avoiding retinoids or sun). We explain downtime and aftercare so you can plan.

Aftercare Notes

Sun protection is critical. We provide specific instructions: gentle cleansing, moisturizing, and avoiding actives or irritation for the period we specify. Do not pick or peel skin; let it shed naturally.

When This May Be a Good Fit

When you want to improve tone, texture, or pigment and can follow aftercare. When you have no conflicting treatments or conditions and can accommodate the downtime we recommend.

When a Consultation Matters Most

First-time peel, sensitive skin, or desire for a deeper peel. We will ensure the right depth and preparation for your safety and results.
