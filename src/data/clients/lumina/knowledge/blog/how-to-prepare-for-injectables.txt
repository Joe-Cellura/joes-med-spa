How to Prepare for Injectables

Lumina Aesthetics — Raleigh, North Carolina

Introduction

A little preparation can help your injectable appointment go smoothly and may reduce the chance of bruising or discomfort. Here’s what we recommend at Lumina.

Before Your Appointment

Avoid blood thinners and certain supplements when possible, as directed by your provider. Common culprits include aspirin, ibuprofen, fish oil, and vitamin E—we will give you a list. If you take a prescribed blood thinner, do not stop it without your doctor’s approval; we will work with you.

Avoid alcohol for 24–48 hours before. Stay hydrated. Get a good night’s sleep. Arrive with a clean face—no makeup in the areas we will treat. If you have a history of cold sores and we are treating around the mouth, we may recommend a preventive medication; tell us in advance.

Day of Treatment

Eat something before you come so you are not lightheaded. Wear comfortable clothing. We will clean the area, may apply topical numbing, and then perform the injections. Plan to avoid rubbing the area, lying flat for a few hours, and strenuous exercise for about 24 hours.

Bruising and Swelling

Even with preparation, some bruising or swelling can occur. It is normal and usually resolves within a few days to a week. If you have an event, we recommend treating at least two weeks ahead so you have time to heal. We will give you aftercare instructions and are available if you have concerns.

Summary

Prepare by avoiding blood thinners and alcohol as we advise, staying hydrated, and arriving with a clean face. Follow aftercare to support the best result.
