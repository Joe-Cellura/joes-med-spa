Botox vs. Filler: What’s the Difference?

Lumina Aesthetics — Raleigh, North Carolina

Introduction

Two of the most common questions we hear: “What’s the difference between Botox and filler?” and “Which one do I need?” Both are injectables, but they work differently. This article explains the basics so you can have a more informed conversation at your consultation.

What Neurotoxins (Botox-Type) Do

Neuromodulators like Botox or Dysport temporarily reduce muscle movement. When you frown, smile, or raise your brows, certain muscles contract and over time can create lines. By relaxing those muscles, we soften the lines that form when you move. The goal is not to freeze your face—it’s to allow natural expression while softening the depth of lines.

What Filler Does

Dermal filler adds volume and can smooth lines that are present even when your face is at rest. It can enhance lips, cheeks, chin, jawline, and soften folds. Filler does not affect muscle; it fills and shapes. Results depend on product choice, placement, and amount.

They Address Different Concerns

If your main concern is lines that appear when you move (dynamic lines), a neurotoxin is usually the first choice. If your concern is lost volume, thin lips, or lines that are visible at rest, filler may be part of the plan. Many clients benefit from both—neurotoxin for movement-related lines and filler for volume or contour—but that is something we decide together at your consultation.

Why a Consultation Matters

Your anatomy, goals, and budget all play a role. We will recommend what makes sense for you and in what order. We do not push both if one is enough. At Lumina we aim for natural, balanced results—so the recommendation is personalized, not one-size-fits-all.
