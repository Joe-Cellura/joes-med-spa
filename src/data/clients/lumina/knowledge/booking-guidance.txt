Lumina Aesthetics — Booking and Timing Guidance

When to Book Before Events or Weddings

If you have a wedding, photo shoot, or major event, plan ahead. For injectables, many providers recommend at least two weeks before the event so any bruising or swelling has time to resolve. For some clients, one month is more comfortable. For facials or Hydrafacial, a few days to a week before is often enough. We can help you choose a treatment and timing that fits your date; we do not recommend rushing in the week of an event for injectables.

Why Consultations Matter

A consultation lets us assess your goals, anatomy, and timeline before any treatment. We can recommend the right service, explain downtime, and give you an accurate estimate. Skipping the consultation increases the chance of a mismatch—wrong treatment, wrong timing, or unmet expectations. We want you to feel informed and confident.

Downtime and Timing

Different treatments have different downtime. Neuromodulators often have minimal downtime; filler can involve a few days of swelling or bruising. Laser and some facials may cause redness for a day or two. We will outline what to expect so you can plan around work, travel, or events. If your schedule is tight, we will suggest options with less downtime or a later treatment date.

When to Reach Out Early

Reach out early if you have a fixed event date, if you are considering a treatment you have never had before, or if you want to build a plan over several months. Booking a consultation a few weeks to a month ahead is usually enough; for events, two to three months ahead gives room to schedule treatment and recovery.

Planning a Gradual Treatment Journey

Many clients prefer to start with one treatment—for example, neuromodulator—and add others over time. We support that approach. A consultation can map out a roadmap: what to do first, what might follow, and how to space visits. There is no obligation to do everything at once.

Follow-Up and Maintenance

Some treatments are one-time or occasional; others benefit from maintenance. We will explain typical follow-up intervals (e.g., neuromodulator every three to four months, or a series of facials). You can book follow-ups as needed; we do not pressure long-term contracts.

Summary

Book a consultation when you are ready to discuss goals and options. For events, book early so we can recommend treatment and timing that works. We will never push you into same-day treatment when a thoughtful plan is better.
