Overview

Professional teeth whitening uses peroxide-based agents to lighten tooth enamel and remove staining from coffee, tea, red wine, tobacco, and natural aging. Results are significantly more effective than over-the-counter products.

Who It's For

Patients with structurally healthy teeth who want a noticeably brighter smile. Best for those with yellow or brown staining from food, beverages, or age. Not effective on dental crowns, veneers, or fillings — those will not lighten with whitening treatment.

Common Concerns

Existing dental restorations will not match whitened natural teeth. We assess this during your consultation and discuss realistic expectations. Patients with significant tooth sensitivity may benefit from a gentler approach.

What Treatment Feels Like

In-office whitening involves applying a professional-strength whitening gel to the teeth and activating it with a specialized light. Most patients experience mild sensitivity during or shortly after treatment, which typically resolves within 24 hours.

Typical Appointment Length

60 to 90 minutes for in-office whitening. Take-home kits involve 30-minute daily sessions over one to two weeks.

Downtime

None. Some patients experience temporary sensitivity to cold or pressure for 24 to 48 hours. Avoiding staining foods and beverages for 48 hours after treatment helps maintain results.

Results Timeline

In-office whitening results are visible immediately. Take-home kit results develop gradually over one to two weeks.

Pricing Range

In-office whitening starts from $299. Take-home kits are typically less expensive. Whitening is considered cosmetic and is not covered by most dental insurance plans.

Consultation Notes

We recommend a cleaning before whitening to remove surface buildup and allow the whitening agent to work evenly. If you have sensitive teeth or existing restorations, mention this at your consultation so we can plan accordingly.

Aftercare

Avoid coffee, tea, red wine, and dark-colored sauces for 48 hours after in-office treatment. Brush with a sensitive toothpaste if needed. Touch-up treatments or take-home trays can extend results.
