Overview

Invisalign uses a series of custom, clear removable aligners to gradually straighten teeth. Each set of aligners is worn for one to two weeks before advancing to the next, moving teeth incrementally toward their target position.

Who It's For

Adults and teens who want to straighten their teeth without traditional metal braces. Invisalign works well for mild to moderate crowding, spacing issues, and some bite problems. Complex orthodontic cases may require traditional braces.

Common Goals

- Straighter teeth without visible metal brackets or wires
- Improved bite alignment
- Closing gaps or correcting crowding
- Better oral hygiene during treatment (aligners are removable)

What Treatment Feels Like

Aligners are smooth, comfortable plastic and fit snugly over the teeth. Each new set creates mild pressure as teeth begin to shift — this is normal and typically fades after a day or two. Most patients adapt to speaking with aligners within a week.

Typical Appointment Length

Check-in visits are typically 20 to 30 minutes every six to eight weeks to monitor progress and receive the next set of aligners.

Downtime

None. Treatment does not interfere with daily activity. Aligners are removed for eating, drinking anything besides water, and brushing.

Results Timeline

Most adult patients complete Invisalign in 12 to 18 months. Simpler cases may finish in as few as six months. Your provider will give a personalized estimate at your consultation.

Pricing Range

Invisalign treatment starts from $3,500. The total cost depends on case complexity and treatment length. Some dental insurance plans offer partial coverage for orthodontic treatment — we verify your benefits before you begin.

Consultation Notes

We offer digital scanning to show a simulation of expected results before you commit. Compliance matters: aligners should be worn 20 to 22 hours per day. Patients who wear them less than recommended will extend their treatment timeline.

Aftercare

After treatment, a retainer is required to maintain your results. Retainer wear schedules are discussed at the end of treatment.
