Overview

Dental implants are titanium posts surgically placed into the jawbone to replace the root of a missing tooth. A crown, bridge, or denture is then attached to restore full function and appearance.

Who It's For

Adults with one or more missing teeth who have sufficient bone density to support an implant. Implants are the most natural-looking and functional long-term solution for tooth replacement.

Common Goals

- Replacing a missing tooth with something that looks and functions like a natural tooth
- Preventing bone loss in the jaw that occurs when teeth are missing
- Avoiding removable dentures or dental bridges
- Restoring confidence in eating, speaking, and smiling

What Treatment Feels Like

The procedure is performed under local anesthesia. Most patients report the surgery is more comfortable than anticipated. After placement, there is typically some soreness, swelling, and mild discomfort for a few days, manageable with over-the-counter pain relief.

Following implant placement, a healing period of three to six months is required before the crown is attached. During this time, a temporary restoration is worn.

Typical Appointment Length

The surgical placement visit is 60 to 90 minutes. The crown placement visit is a separate appointment after healing.

Downtime

Plan for a day or two of rest after surgery. Soft foods are recommended for the first week. Avoid smoking, which significantly impairs healing.

Results Timeline

Final crown placement occurs three to six months after implant surgery, once the implant has fully integrated with the bone.

Pricing Range

Dental implants start from $3,000 per implant, which typically includes the implant post, abutment, and crown. Implants are generally not covered by dental insurance, though some plans offer partial benefits. We discuss financing options for patients who need them.

Consultation Notes

Candidates must have healthy gums and adequate bone to support an implant. Uncontrolled diabetes, heavy smoking, and certain medications may affect candidacy. A full exam and X-rays are required to determine eligibility.

Longevity

With proper care, implants can last a lifetime. The crown on top typically lasts 10 to 15 years before it may need replacement.
