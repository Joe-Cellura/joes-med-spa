Overview

Porcelain veneers are thin, custom-crafted shells bonded to the front surface of the teeth to improve their color, shape, length, and alignment. They are one of the most comprehensive cosmetic dental treatments available.

Who It's For

Patients who want to transform the overall appearance of their smile. Veneers address a wide range of concerns: significant discoloration that whitening cannot correct, chipped or worn teeth, minor gaps or crowding, teeth that are uneven in size or shape, and more.

Common Goals

- A dramatically whiter, more uniform smile
- Correction of chips, cracks, or worn edges
- Closing small gaps between teeth
- Reshaping teeth that are too short, long, or narrow
- Achieving a natural-looking cosmetic result

What Treatment Feels Like

The process typically involves two to three visits. The first is a consultation and planning session. The second involves minimal tooth preparation (a thin layer of enamel is removed to create space for the veneer) and placement of temporary veneers while the permanent ones are crafted in a lab. The final visit is the bonding appointment.

Typical Appointment Length

Each visit is 60 to 90 minutes. The full process spans two to three weeks.

Downtime

Minimal. Some patients experience sensitivity during the preparation phase. Temporaries are worn in between visits and function normally.

Results Timeline

Final veneers are placed at the last visit and results are immediate.

Pricing Range

Porcelain veneers start from $1,200 per tooth. The total cost depends on the number of teeth treated. Veneers are a cosmetic procedure and are not covered by dental insurance.

Consultation Notes

Not every patient is a candidate for veneers. Active gum disease, significant tooth decay, and heavy grinding habits must be addressed before proceeding. We conduct a thorough exam and discuss candidacy honestly at your consultation.

Longevity and Care

With proper care, porcelain veneers typically last 10 to 20 years. Avoid biting nails, chewing ice, or opening packages with your teeth. Wear a nightguard if you grind. Continue regular cleanings every six months.
