Overview

Bright Smile Dental reserves appointment time each day for patients experiencing dental emergencies. If you are in pain or have a sudden dental problem, call us as early as possible and we will do our best to see you same day.

What Counts as a Dental Emergency

- Severe tooth pain that is constant or prevents sleep
- A broken, chipped, or knocked-out tooth
- A lost filling or crown
- A dental abscess (swelling, throbbing, or visible infection)
- Significant swelling of the jaw or face
- Bleeding that does not stop after applying pressure

What Is Not Typically an Emergency

Mild tooth sensitivity, routine discomfort after a procedure, or a loose retainer are usually not emergencies. Call us and describe what you are experiencing — we can help you determine whether you need to come in right away.

What to Do If a Tooth Is Knocked Out

Time matters. Rinse the tooth gently without scrubbing, hold it by the crown (not the root), and try to reinsert it into the socket if possible. If not, store it in milk or between your cheek and gum and get to the office immediately. Successful reimplantation is most likely within the first 30 to 60 minutes.

Typical Appointment Length

Varies depending on the nature of the emergency.

Pricing

Emergency visit fees vary based on what treatment is required. Call us and we will give you a sense of what to expect. We provide clear pricing before beginning any treatment.

After Hours

If you experience a dental emergency outside of office hours, call our main number. Our voicemail includes guidance for urgent situations. In the event of significant swelling or difficulty breathing, go to the nearest emergency room.

Consultation Notes

Emergency visits address the immediate issue. A follow-up appointment is usually recommended to address any underlying cause and discuss long-term treatment options.
