Overview

Professional teeth cleaning (prophylaxis) is one of the most important preventive services in dentistry. It removes plaque and tartar that regular brushing and flossing cannot reach, reducing the risk of cavities, gum disease, and tooth loss.

Who It's For

Most adults and children who want to maintain their oral health. Patients with no active dental disease benefit from cleanings every six months. Those with a history of gum disease or higher tartar buildup may need more frequent visits.

What Treatment Feels Like

A hygienist uses hand instruments and an ultrasonic scaler to remove buildup above and below the gumline. The teeth are then polished with a mild abrasive paste. Most patients find the experience comfortable. Some sensitivity is normal near the gumline.

Typical Appointment Length

45 to 60 minutes, including a brief exam by the dentist.

Downtime

None. Patients can eat and drink normally after the appointment. Minor gum sensitivity may last a day or two.

Results Timeline

Your teeth will feel noticeably cleaner immediately after. Regular cleanings over time reduce staining and help maintain a brighter appearance.

Pricing Range

Cleanings typically start from $150 for a standard adult prophylaxis, not including X-rays. Insurance covers cleanings at varying rates depending on your plan.

Aftercare

Continue regular brushing twice daily and flossing once daily. Rinse with water after consuming staining beverages like coffee or red wine. Return for your next scheduled cleaning as recommended.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often do I need a cleaning?
A: Every six months for most patients. More frequently if you have a history of gum disease.

Q: Does it hurt?
A: Most patients experience little to no discomfort. If your gums are inflamed or sensitive, your hygienist will work carefully and can adjust the pressure.

Q: Can a cleaning whiten my teeth?
A: It removes surface staining and polishes the enamel, which can improve brightness slightly. For more significant whitening, professional whitening treatment is recommended.
