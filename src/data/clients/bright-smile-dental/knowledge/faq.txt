Frequently Asked Questions — Bright Smile Dental

General

Q: How often should I get a cleaning?
A: Most patients benefit from professional cleanings every six months. Patients with a history of gum disease or higher risk of tartar buildup may need visits every three to four months. Your hygienist will make a personalized recommendation at your first visit.

Q: Who performs dental treatments at Bright Smile?
A: All treatments are performed by licensed dental professionals. Dr. James Park handles general and restorative dentistry. Dr. Maya Patel leads cosmetic work including veneers and whitening. Sarah Kim, RDH, performs cleanings and periodontal care.

Q: Do you take new patients?
A: Yes. We welcome new patients and offer consultations to discuss your oral health goals and treatment options.

Q: Do you see patients with dental anxiety?
A: Absolutely. Many of our patients come to us after difficult experiences at other practices. We work at your pace and will never pressure you into treatment you are not ready for. We also offer local anesthesia and comfort options for procedures that cause anxiety.

Cosmetic Dentistry

Q: Is whitening safe?
A: Yes. Professional whitening performed or supervised by a dentist is safe and effective. We assess your candidacy before recommending a whitening option and advise on timing to minimize sensitivity.

Q: How long do veneers last?
A: Porcelain veneers typically last 10 to 20 years with proper care, including regular cleanings and avoiding habits like nail-biting or chewing hard objects.

Q: Will my results look natural?
A: Natural-looking outcomes are a priority for every treatment we provide. Whether you are getting veneers, whitening, or implants, the goal is a smile that looks like the best version of yours — not an obvious transformation.

Q: Is Invisalign right for adults?
A: Yes. Invisalign is commonly used by adults who want to straighten their teeth without traditional metal braces. Most treatment plans run 12 to 18 months. A consultation can show you a digital simulation of expected results before you commit.

Restorative Dentistry

Q: How long do dental implants last?
A: Implants are designed to be permanent. With good oral hygiene and routine dental care, they can last a lifetime. The crown placed on top of the implant typically lasts 10 to 15 years before it may need to be replaced.

Q: Is the implant procedure painful?
A: The procedure is performed under local anesthesia. Most patients report that the procedure itself is far more comfortable than they anticipated. Some soreness and swelling in the days following are normal and manageable with over-the-counter pain relief.

Pricing and Insurance

Q: Do you provide pricing upfront?
A: Yes. You will receive a clear written treatment plan with pricing before any work begins. We do not start treatment without your informed consent.

Q: Do you accept insurance?
A: We work with most major dental insurance plans. Contact us before your visit and we can verify your coverage and explain what to expect in terms of out-of-pocket costs.

Q: What if I don't have insurance?
A: We offer a clear self-pay fee schedule and payment plan options. Ask us about financing when you call or come in.

Q: Can I be seen the same day as my consultation?
A: For cleanings, simple exams, and minor procedures, same-day treatment is often possible if time permits. For more involved work like veneers, implants, or Invisalign, treatment is scheduled at a follow-up visit after your plan is finalized.

Emergency Care

Q: What counts as a dental emergency?
A: Severe tooth pain, a broken or knocked-out tooth, a lost filling or crown, a dental abscess, or significant swelling are all reasons to call us right away. We reserve appointment slots each day for urgent needs.

Q: What if I need care outside business hours?
A: If you have a dental emergency after hours, call our main number. Our voicemail includes guidance for true emergencies and the nearest emergency dental resource if needed.
